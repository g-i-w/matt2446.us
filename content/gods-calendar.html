God's Calendar

69/70 Ratio

Ancient calendars used a 360-day year and some continue to do so.  Did the Flood cause Earth's orbital period to change?  It's possible that catastrophic changes in Earth's crust during the Flood caused a change in Earth's orbital period from the initial 360-day year to the current length of a year.  But was this change random or did God divinely ordain an exact ratio?

(69/70) / (360/365.24219) = 1.0000679

In fact, the ratio 69/70 almost exactly relates a 360-day year to the current Solar year with a discrepancy of only 36 minutes.  There are multiple ways to measure a year and Earth's axial precession causes the length of a year to vary up to 30 minutes.  Accepting the current Mean Tropical Solar Year of 365.24219 days, which is based on a model, the 69/70 ratio is accurate to within 7 thousandths of 1 percent (70 ppm).  This is comparable to the tolerance of a quartz crystal oscillator used in a digital clock.  It's safe to say this 69/70 ratio could not occur by chance!

The unit of feet, passed down to the Greeks and Romans from the ancient Egyptians, appears to have been defined from a relationship between 360 and the length of a year.  If you're at the equator, a distance of 1/360 of Earth's circumference (also known as 1° of longitude) is 1000 feet multiplied by the length of a year.

62/70 Ratio

A 62/70 ratio closely relates the Full Moon Cycle (411.78443029 days) to the current Solar year.  A 69/70 ratio closely relates the Lunar Year (354.36707 days) to a 360-day year.  There is a half-day discrepancy in both Lunar ratios, which may relate to night-only observability of the Moon.  In any case, the Lunar ratios are accurate to at least 0.1%, which is more than statistically significant.

Which Command and What Year?

Depending on who you listen to, the command to restore and rebuild Jerusalem might have been in 457 BC, 449 BC, or 444 BC.  One interesting result of these ratios is that no matter which calendar you happen to be using and which command event you use for a starting point, there are durations of either 62, 69, or 70 weeks of years pointing to significance right around the year that Jesus, Son of God, the Messiah was cut off, for you and I.

360-day Year 444 BC (69 * 7 * 360 days) - (444 - 1 year) 33 AD
Current Solar Year 449 BC (69 * 7 years) - (449 - 1 year) 35 AD
360-day Year 449 BC (70 * 7 * 360 days) - (449 - 1 year) 35 AD
411.78-day Lunar Cycle 457 BC (62 * 7 * 411.78443 days) - (457 - 1 year) 33 AD
Current Solar Year 457 BC (70 * 7 years) - (457 - 1 year) 34 AD

Why the extra -1 year? There is no "0 year", so we need to subtract one year.

6 Days of History and the 7th Day

One of the hints indicating the 7th millennium or Day of the Lord is coming soon, at the conclusion of the last 2 days between Jesus' Ascension and Return, is the obvious correlation of history to 6000 years or 6 days.  But if God ordained 6000 years, shouldn't it be exact?  James Ussher, Johannes Kepler, and Isaac Newton each calculated the date of Creation to be very close to 4000 years (± 4 years) before the Birth of Jesus.  If Jesus will return 2000 years after He ascended, how can we account for 30 years + 3.5 years between Jesus' Birth and Ascension?  This 33.5 year segment is large enough to be outside the ± 4 year tolerance.

Possible meanings for the 33.5 year segment:

  • It could highlight the interlude where God "paused" the stop-watch of His-Story.
  • It could be the time Adam lived a sinless life in the Garden, before the Fall.
  • It could be related to the change in orbital period at the Flood, which can account for 33.5 years. How?  Keep reading.

During the interval between the date of the Flood (1656 years after Creation) and the 4000 year mark, the 360-day calendar and the current Solar calendar reach a discrepancy of 33.49 years (or 33.64 years):

(4000-1656)-(4000-1656)*(69/70) = 33.49

If this is correct, Jesus' Birth is 4000 "perfect" 360-day years after Creation and He was crucified and rose again 4000 Solar years after Creation.  During the first 1656 years, 360-day years and Solar years would be the same, then after the Flood, Solar years would lag behind "perfect" 360-day years.  In truth, this timeline could be exactly consistent with the Ussher's date of Creation because of the basic assumptions his date relies on.
